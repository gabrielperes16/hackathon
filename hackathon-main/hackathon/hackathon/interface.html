Planets of the Solar System

Sol - Sun - The star that sustains our entire solar system. A gigantic ball of plasma emitting light and heat, essential for life on Earth.
Mercury - The closest planet to the Sun, with extreme temperatures and deep craters. Although it is the smallest planet in the solar system, its proximity to the Sun makes it a place of extreme conditions.
Venus - Known as Earth’s "sister planet" due to its similar size, but with a completely inhospitable environment. Its dense atmosphere, rich in carbon dioxide, makes it the hottest planet in the solar system.
Earth - The only known planet that harbors life, with an atmosphere rich in oxygen and abundant liquid water. Home to millions of species, including humans.
Mars - The mysterious "Red Planet," known for its desert landscapes and towering mountains. It has evidence that it once had liquid water on its surface and is the main target of exploration missions.
Jupiter - The gas giant and largest planet in the solar system. Its atmosphere is marked by violent storms, including the iconic Great Red Spot, a storm that has lasted for centuries.
Saturn - Famous for its majestic rings made of ice and dust, Saturn is the second-largest planet in the solar system. Its rings can even be seen with small telescopes from Earth.
Uranus - A mysterious planet with an unusual rotation. Uranus spins almost on its side, with its axis tilted 98 degrees, making it unique in the solar system.
Neptune - The farthest planet from the Sun, known for its extremely strong winds and turbulent weather. It is the last of the gas giants and one of the coldest planets in the solar system.
